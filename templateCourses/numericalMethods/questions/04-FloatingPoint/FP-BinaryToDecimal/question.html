In the IEEE 754 floating point standard (for double precision), machine numbers are written in the following form: $$ \underbrace{0}_\text{s} \underbrace{10100110010}_\text{c} \underbrace{10100100110010\cdot \cdot \cdot 0}_\text{f} $$ Where the $\cdot \cdot \cdot $ implies the intermediate digits are all zero.

To convert to a (base ten) decimal representation, we use the following formula: $$ (-1)^s \times (1.f) \times 2^{c - 1023} $$ Note that $c$ and $f$ must be converted to decimal numbers before applying this formula. $c$ is an integer number and therefore the right most digit of its binary representation corresponds to $2^0$, with the powers of $2$ increasing to the left. $f$ is the fractional part of the significand.

Find the decimal representation of the two following machine numbers.

a) ${{params.machine_number_1}}$

b) ${{params.machine_number_2}}$
