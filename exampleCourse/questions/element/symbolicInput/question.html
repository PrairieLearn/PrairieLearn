Enabling non-default sets of functions by group name:

We will give an example where a few of the allowed function group names are enabled. The next listings document what named function definitions are included by enabling these individual groups:

  • calculus-extended: Support for definitions of function names and implementations that are common in problem sets for typical introductory courses in differential and integral calculus including $\cos$, $\sin$, $\tan$, $\sec$, $\cot$, $\csc$, $\operatorname{acos}$, $\operatorname{asin}$, $\operatorname{atan}$, $\operatorname{asec}$, $\operatorname{acot}$, $\operatorname{acsc}$, $\operatorname{arccos}$, $\operatorname{arcsin}$, $\operatorname{arctan}$, $\operatorname{atan2}$, $\cosh$, $\sinh$, $\tanh$, $\operatorname{sech}$, $\coth$, $\operatorname{csch}$, $\operatorname{acosh}$, $\operatorname{asinh}$, $\operatorname{atanh}$, $\operatorname{asech}$, $\operatorname{acoth}$, $\operatorname{acsch}$, $\ln$, and $\exp$.
  • complex-variables: Support for definitions of standard functions used with complex variables including $\operatorname{arg}$, $\operatorname{mod}$, $\operatorname{re}$, $\operatorname{im}$, and $\operatorname{conj}$.
  • logarithmic: Support for definitions of common variants on notation for a few logarithm type functions including $\log$, $\ln$, $\operatorname{lg}$, $\operatorname{log2}$, $\operatorname{log10}$, $\exp$.
  • prog-extended: Support for definitions of functions that are otherwise common in discrete math and / or theory classes including $\max$, $\min$, $\operatorname{ceil}$, $\operatorname{floor}$, $\operatorname{frac}$, $\operatorname{abs}$, $\operatorname{sgn}$, $\gcd$, $\operatorname{lcm}$, and $\operatorname{sqrt}$.
  • trig-base: Support for definitions of basic (non-hyperbolic) trigonometric functions including $\cos$, $\sin$, $\tan$, $\sec$, $\cot$, $\csc$, $\operatorname{acos}$, $\operatorname{asin}$, $\operatorname{atan}$, $\operatorname{asec}$, $\operatorname{acot}$, $\operatorname{acsc}$, $\operatorname{arccos}$, $\operatorname{arcsin}$, $\operatorname{arctan}$, and $\operatorname{atan2}$.
  • trig-hyperbolic: Support for definitions of hyperbolic trig functions and their inverses including $\cosh$, $\sinh$, $\tanh$, $\operatorname{sech}$, $\coth$, $\operatorname{csch}$, $\operatorname{acosh}$, $\operatorname{asinh}$, $\operatorname{atanh}$, $\operatorname{asech}$, $\operatorname{acoth}$, and $\operatorname{acsch}$.
