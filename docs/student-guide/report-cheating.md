# Reporting suspected cheating during an exam

During some exams administered through PrairieTest, you can privately tell exam staff if you see someone breaking the exam rules. For example, you might see someone using a phone or unauthorized materials.

When reporting is available, the **Report cheating** button appears at the top of your PrairieLearn assessment while you are taking the exam.

## Submit a report

1. Click **Report cheating**.
2. Describe what you saw. Include details that will help exam staff understand what happened and identify the situation.
3. Click **Submit report**.

PrairieLearn will let you know when your report has been submitted. Your name is included with the report, and exam staff can review it during and after the exam. Other students will not see it.

## If your report cannot be submitted

If PrairieLearn cannot confirm that your report was submitted, your description will remain in the form so you can try again. If the problem continues, or if the situation needs immediate attention, tell a proctor directly.

The reporting option is not available during every exam. If you do not see the **Report cheating** button, tell a proctor directly.

For questions about the exam or help with a technical problem, contact a proctor instead of using a cheating report.
